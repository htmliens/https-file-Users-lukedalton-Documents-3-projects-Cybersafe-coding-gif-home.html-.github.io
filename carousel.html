Online Health

The internet is new for all of us so it’s hard to know what bad habits we are forming and what to watch out for. So let’s take a look at what could be harmful and what good behaviour we should be practicing.

Make it worthwhile

Although it is important to be gauging how much screen time is the right amount the focus should be on ‘quality’ screen-time. There is an unlimited amount of media online for our children to access, try to ensure that your children are watching worthwhile or educating videos as there is a wealth of great content online too. Spending time video chatting family members is another wholesome use of the internet.

Hatch a plan

Figuring out what’s right for your family is the key and it’s crucial that this time does not encroach on sleep cycle, physical activity, socialising and other requirements of healthy living. The key is to find the right balance of time and to consistently apply colours. Check out our no-phone zones and offline times below. If these rules are written up and displayed in the house you’ll find it easier to remember and stick to them, but you must follow them too!

Pick your own location

Decide what’s an important time for your children to be paying attention to their surroundings. Maybe it’s at their grandparents house or when walking in public.

Bedroom

If your children are on their computers in their rooms they’re more likely to explore riskier content online. If you keep the laptop in the communal living space you can keep an eye on them much easier!
