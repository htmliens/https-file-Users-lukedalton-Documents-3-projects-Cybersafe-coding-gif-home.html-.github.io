Start the Conversation

By using these conversation starters we can encourage children to talk about their time online just as they would any other aspect of their life. Ask one of them today!

Talk about what they like doing

Ask them what apps they like to use, what famous people they like to follow and what they’re watching on youtube.

Ask what they see online that they worry about

It’s important to get them talking about things that might seem scary or intimidating to them. Ask what they would do if they found something online that made them feel upset or worried.

Ask them for their own top tips

Asking for their help can gauge their knowledge but also open the door to discussing ideas further.

Go through the privacy settings on their apps

Take a look at the privacy settings on their social media and messaging apps. (e.g. the options that set out who can see their photos or follow them) Ask them how they would repeat or block someone that makes them feel uncomfortable or upset.

Ask them for help

Ask your child to help you do something online such as changing the privacy settings on your social media account, search for information on something or downloading an app. It helps to get an idea of how proficient they are with tech and this can open up other avenues of conversation.
